Guide to Required Documents for Municipal Services

To apply for services in Cantilan, consult the Citizen's Charter (available at the Municipal Hall or provincial website) or visit visitcantilan.com. Cantilan, a coastal town with 17 barangays and a population of 34,060, follows standard LGU guidelines under Surigao del Sur ordinances. Below is a detailed guide to common requirements, processing times, and fees for business and personal applications (as of September 2025; subject to local updates).

Business-Related Transactions

Requirements for permits and licenses, such as business permits or occupancy permits, as per Surigao del Sur Revenue Code and Municipal Ordinance No. 2023-015 (or latest equivalent):

  • SEC/DTI/CDA Registration (updated certificate from the Department of Trade and Industry)
  • Fire Safety Inspection Certificate (issued by the Bureau of Fire Protection, Cantilan Station)
  • Sanitary Permit (from the Municipal Health Office in Cantilan)
  • Building Permit (for new constructions or renovations, from the Municipal Engineering Office)
  • Barangay Business Clearance (from the specific barangay in Cantilan, e.g., Magosilom or Baybay)
  • Payment of Local Taxes and Fees (based on gross annual receipts; includes environmental clearance for coastal areas)
  • Community Tax Certificate (Cedula) and valid ID
More Info on Business Permits in Cantilan

Processing Time: 3-5 working days upon complete submission.
Fees: ₱500 - ₱5,000 (depending on business type, size, and location; e.g., higher for coastal fishing-related businesses).
Where to Submit: Business Permits and Licensing Office (BPLO), Municipal Hall, Brgy. Magosilom, Cantilan.
Additional Notes: For mining-related businesses (common in Surigao del Sur), include DENR clearance. Late renewals incur 25% surcharge.

Personal Applications

Requirements for services like certifications, clearances, indigency, or government appointments:

  • Government-Issued ID (e.g., Passport, Driver’s License, PhilID, or UMID)
  • Proof of Address (e.g., recent utility bill, Barangay Certificate of Residency from a Cantilan barangay)
  • LGU Certification of Availability of Funds (for government appointments, issued by the Municipal Budget Office)
  • Additional documents specific to the service (e.g., Birth/Marriage Certificate for civil registry; Affidavit of Indigency for free services)
  • Two recent passport-sized photos (for some applications)
More Info on Personal Certifications in Cantilan

Processing Time: 1-3 working days for most certifications (e.g., Residency or No Marriage Record).
Fees: ₱50 - ₱300 per document (waived for indigents; e.g., ₱100 for Certificate of Good Moral Character).
Where to Submit: Office of the Municipal Civil Registrar or Barangay Hall (e.g., in Poblacion or Antao).
Additional Notes: For coastal residents, include proof of residency for disaster-related clearances (common due to Pacific Ocean exposure).

How to Access the Citizen's Charter in Cantilan

The Citizen's Charter outlines all services, steps, and fees for Cantilan's 17 barangays. It aligns with national standards and local ordinances.

Accessing the Citizen's Charter

Online: Check the provincial site at surigaodelsur.gov.ph or Facebook page for updates.
Physical Copy: Available at the Public Information Desk, Municipal Hall, Ortega St., Brgy. Magosilom.
Updates: Reviewed annually; latest as of 2025 includes post-pandemic adjustments for services.
Feedback: Report issues to the LGU for improvements under RA 9485.
